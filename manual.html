§ 01

Game Overview

Gaze Pattern Shooter is a mobile WebVR game where you use only your gaze to identify and destroy colour-matching balls in a 3D grid. A target ball is shown in the lower-centre of your view — find every ball in the grid that matches its colour and stare at each one to fire a projectile. Clear all matching balls to advance through waves and rounds.

The game runs entirely in the browser and requires no installation beyond a WebVR-capable phone and a Google Cardboard (or equivalent) headset. It can also be played on a desktop browser using mouse-look.

No Controller Needed Works on Mobile Chrome Google Cardboard Compatible Mouse-Look on Desktop

Setup & Installation

Option A — Play from GitHub Pages (recommended)

No installation required. Open the live link in your browser:

https://ma-452.github.io/MatchShot/

Option B — Run locally

  1. Download the repository Click Code → Download ZIP on GitHub, or clone with git clone https://github.com/MA-452/MatchShot.git
  2. Serve the files over HTTP A local server is required for audio to load. If you have Python installed, run the command below from the project folder:
  3. Open in browser Navigate to http://localhost:8000 in Chrome or Firefox.
cd path/to/project python -m http.server 8000
⚠
Do not open index.html directly as a file:// URL — audio and some textures will fail due to browser security restrictions. Always use a local server or GitHub Pages.

Mobile VR setup (Google Cardboard)

  1. Open the game URL on your phone Use Chrome on Android or Safari on iOS. Make sure the page is fully loaded before inserting your phone.
  2. Tap "⬡ ENTER VR" The button appears at the bottom of the screen. This switches to stereoscopic split-screen mode.
  3. Insert phone into headset Place your phone in a Google Cardboard or compatible VR viewer. The game now responds to head movement.
  4. Use gaze to navigate all menus Point the centre dot (reticle) at a button and hold for 2 seconds to activate it. No tapping needed.

§ 03

Controls & Input

The game is designed to be played hands-free using gaze (fuse-based cursor). A white ring reticle at the centre of your view acts as your only input device.

Action Mobile VR Desktop
Look around Move your head Click + drag, or arrow keys
Activate button / shoot ball Centre reticle on target and hold for 2 sec Move reticle with mouse onto target, hold for 2 sec
Hover feedback Ball scales up when reticle enters its hitbox Same
Enter VR mode Tap the ⬡ ENTER VR button on screen Click the VR goggles icon (bottom-right of canvas)
Hard mode fuse Hold reticle for only 1.2 sec Same
Wave 2 fuse (all difficulties) Fuse timeout decreases by 400 ms from base Same
💡
Reticle tip: Each ball has an invisible hitbox larger than its visible surface. You do not need to aim at the exact centre — anywhere within the ball's general area will trigger the hover and fire.

§ 04

How to Play

  1. Flash Screen The title screen appears when the game loads. Gaze at [ GAZE TO START ] and hold for 2 seconds to proceed.
  2. Setup Menu Choose your difficulty (Easy or Hard) and whether background music plays. Gaze at [ BEGIN ] to start.
  3. Identify the target colour A textured ball labelled TARGET appears in the lower-centre of your view. Remember its colour and pattern.
  4. Shoot matching balls Find every ball in the grid that matches the target colour. Gaze at each one to fire a projectile. Correct hits score +10 points and destroy the ball. Wrong colour hits cost -5 points and one life.
  5. Wave 2 begins automatically Once all matching balls are cleared, Wave 2 starts. A new target colour is chosen from an expanded set of 6 colours and the fuse timer gets shorter.
  6. Round complete Clearing Wave 2 ends the round. A new round begins with a fresh grid. There are 5 rounds total.
  7. Win or lose Clear all 5 rounds to win. Lose all 3 lives or run out of time to get Game Over. Gaze at [ PLAY AGAIN ] to return to Setup.

§ 05

Levels & Difficulty

Each of the 5 rounds contains two waves. Difficulty affects grid size and how long you must hold your gaze to fire.

Wave 01 — all rounds

Primary Wave

Grid uses 5 colours (red, blue, green, purple, orange). At least 3 balls always match the target. Base fuse timeout applies.

Wave 02 — all rounds

Surge Wave

Pink is added as a 6th colour. Fuse timeout is reduced by 400 ms. A new target is chosen from all 6 colours.

Difficulty Grid Size Base Fuse Wave 2 Fuse
EASY 3 rows × 4 cols (12 balls) 2.0 s 1.6 s
HARD 4 rows × 6 cols (24 balls) 1.2 s 0.8 s

§ 06

HUD & Scoring

The HUD is always visible once the game starts. It is attached to the camera so it follows your gaze and remains readable in VR mode.

❤️
Lives (top-right)

You start with 3 lives. Each wrong-colour shot costs one life. Losing all 3 ends the game immediately.

★
Score (below lives)

Running total. Displayed in green. Score never drops below 0.

🔄
Round (top-left)

Shows current round out of 5, e.g. Round: 2/5.

⏱
Timer (below round)

2:00 countdown. Turns red in the final 15 seconds. Running out of time ends the game.

Point Values
Correct colour shot (match) + 10
Wrong colour shot (mismatch) − 5  &  − 1 life
Minimum score 0 (cannot go negative)

§ 07

Tips & Tricks

🎯
Memorise the target before scanning. The target ball sits in the lower-centre of your view. Study its colour and the dot pattern before looking up at the grid.
⚡
Scan systematically. Work row by row across the grid rather than jumping around. This prevents missing or double-shooting a ball.
🔴
When in doubt, skip it. A wrong shot costs a life. With only 3 lives, a couple of mistakes can end an otherwise perfect run. If you are not sure about a colour, move on and return.
🎵
Music requires a user gesture. Background music starts when you click BEGIN. If it does not start immediately, tap anywhere on the screen once — browsers require a touch or click before playing audio.
📱
Allow device orientation. On iOS, the browser may ask permission to access the gyroscope. Tap Allow when prompted, or go to Settings → Safari → Motion & Orientation Access and enable it.
🥽
Adjust your headset IPD. If the stereoscopic image looks blurry or doubled, adjust the lens separation on your Cardboard viewer until the two images merge cleanly.
